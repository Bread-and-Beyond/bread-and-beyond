title: "Challah"
category: breads

tags: ["bread", "shabbat", "holiday"]
image: "/bread-and-beyond/images/recipes/challah.jpg"
dietary: "Parve"
prepTime: "30 min"
cookTime: "30 min"
servings: "6 loaves"
publishDate: 2026-07-07
ingredientGroups:
  - heading: "For proofing the yeast:"
    items:
      - "4 tablespoons active dry yeast (37.2 g)"
      - "5 cups warm water (40 ounces)"
      - "2 tablespoons granulated sugar (25 g)"
  - heading: "For the dough:"
    items:
      - "5 lbs high-gluten flour, plus more as needed"
      - "1 cup granulated sugar (200 g)"
      - "2 tablespoons salt (36 g)"
      - "3 eggs"
      - "1/4 cup honey (85 g)"
      - "1 1/4 cups olive oil (267 g)"
steps:
  - "Combine the active dry yeast, warm water, and 2 tablespoons sugar in a glass bowl. Set aside to proof for 10 minutes."
  - "In a very large bowl, combine almost all of the high-gluten flour with 1 cup sugar and the salt."
  - "Add the eggs, honey, and olive oil, then mix well."
  - "Once the yeast is proofed, add it to the flour mixture and mix until all ingredients are well incorporated."
  - "Transfer the dough to a work surface and knead until smooth and elastic, adding more flour as needed."
  - "Return the dough to the large bowl. Cover with plastic wrap and a towel, then set aside to rise for 1 hour and 30 minutes in a warm, draft-free spot."
  - "Preheat the oven to 350°F."
  - "Uncover the dough and take challah. Portion and braid the challahs."
  - "Let the braided challahs rise for 30 minutes."
  - "Brush with egg wash, sprinkle with toppings, and bake for about 30 minutes."
  - "Let cool completely before storing."
